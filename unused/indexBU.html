IRON WOOOOO-MAAAN!

Iron Woman has long been a favorite cover of ours. Originally by Devin Davis, who's sole album "Lonely People of the World Unite" is a criminally underrated power-pop gem. It's been a staple of our live shows for years.

A bunch of years ago we realized that we were all in the same Devin Davis appreciation facebook group so, covering one of his songs was an easy move.

The recent downtime in live music has been a good opportunity to record our own version of it and use the proceeds to support a cause we believe in (women's reproductive rights). All proceeds go to Planned Parenthood.

The track features a bunch of talented buds: Joel Reader (The Fatal Flaw, MTX, Pansy Division), Jac Mestrel (Little Happy Clouds), John Drislane (The Daisies, The Maxims), Elio DeLuca (Titus Andronicus) and more!

Did I mention there is a sax solo? Hell yeah!

WELCOME TO ISLAND TOWN

The first single off the Sinnet's forthcoming suburban, dad-rock, mini-concept, album, Island Town. The song is my scrapbook of the common American experience of buying a house, starting a family and moving to the suburbs. Despite an insane real estate market we managed to find a lovely little home in Medford, MA. Initially we felt a bit removed from the world but, soon found contentment in the unique quirks, the bathtub Virgin Marys, boats on lawns, even the squirrels tearing up our yard.

Written, performed and recorded by A. Spransy at Gentle Humor Recording Medford, MA. Mix assistance and mastering by Matthew Girard.

NEW EP & RELEASE SHOW

TENNIS ELBOW CLUB - OUT NOW

Tennis Elbow Club is our new 4 song EP! This collection of songs is our most personal, textured and nuanced to date. It celebrates my love of songwriters like Paul Simon and Elliott Smith with the rhythm and swagger of bands like Booker T & The MGs and Spoon. It blends elements of soul, surf, synth-pop and spookiness with smart indie-rock and pop.

Thematically, the songs continue the emotional and personal catharsis of the project and center around themes of family, memory and keeping the memory of my mother alive.

This marks our first time recording with Mr. Elio DeLuca at Soul Shop in Medford, MA. Elio has worked with countless Boston bands as well as renown acts like Titus Andronicus, Wilder Maker, Peter Matthew Bauer and many more. He brought a seriously deep knowledge of music, recording and silly 90's hip hop references to our jams.
